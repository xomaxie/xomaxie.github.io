GateStore

Story & Design Bible

Ley-Net Ley-Energy GateDrop™ MVP + Stretch

1. Premise

GateStore is the general & adventurer supply shop perched on a leyline nexus—magical currents that power the city, carry information, and occasionally spit out a goat.

Leylines are the backbone of modern life: the Ley-Net (instant magical communications & commerce) and Ley-Energy (clean, renewable, pleasantly sparkly power). Your job is to keep shelves stocked, customers happy, and the GateDrop portal from flattening anyone during deliveries.

2. World & Tone

  • Fantasy meets everyday life: enchanted swords beside enchanted frying pans.
  • Goofy but consistent: yes, the GateDrop once delivered an entire cow; no one blinked.
  • Alive & bustling: gossiping adventurers, ley-tea complaints, courier arguments with the portal.

3. Leyline Infrastructure

  • Ley-Energy: powers lamps to coffee warmers; sometimes makes the mop float.
  • Ley-Net: global arcane network for messaging, ordering stock, and highly educational cat videos.
  • GateDrop™ Portal: brass-and-crystal ring outside the shop. Small orders arrive in crates; big orders thunk to the pavement; sometimes the wrong thing comes through.

4. The Shop

Owner: You.

  • Shop Floor: displays & sales.
  • Storage: where boxes pile up until you deal with them.
  • Ley-Terminal Desk: your magical computer (with questionable pop-up ads).

5. Products

MVP Stock

  • Adventurer Gear: starter swords, shields, bows; health & mana potions; cheap talismans (“Guaranteed to work… once”).
  • Civilian Goods: bread, fruit, ley-tea; cooking pots; enchanted brooms that sometimes chase cats.

Stretch Goal Stock

  • Rare weapons & armor (e.g., “Thudbringer”, “Soggy Blade”).
  • Crafted items made from dungeon loot.
  • Ley-trinkets for mood, luck, and questionable romance.

6. Stock Sources

MVP

  • Ley-Net Orders via Ley-Terminal → delivered through GateDrop.
  • Adventurer Buybacks: they sell dungeon loot, you haggle, you resell.

Future Additions

  • Leyline Drift Finds—random rare goods from unstable currents.
  • Guild Contracts—bulk orders for expeditions.
  • Artifact Auctions—bid against rivals.

7. Currency

Leymarks (ℒ): crystal‑infused coinage that faintly glows and smells of cinnamon.

  • MVP: only Leymarks.
  • Future: dungeon tokens, rare reagents, influential “favors”.

8. Gameplay Loop (MVP)

  1. Check stock.
  2. Order via Ley-Net.
  3. Wait for GateDrop to whomp the delivery outside.
  4. Place items on shelves.
  5. Sell to customers.
  6. Count Leymarks, sweep stray portal deliveries, sleep.

9. Events

MVP

  • Busy/Slow Day demand modifiers.
  • Occasional GateDrop mix-up (minor gag items).

Future Events

  • Leyline Surges: more customers, rare loot, odd effects.
  • Rift Days: corrupted goods appear.
  • GateDrop Malfunction: too much stuff at once—or from the wrong dimension.

10. NPC Types

MVP

  • Adventurers: buy gear, sell loot.
  • Townsfolk: groceries & home goods.
  • GateDrop Couriers: “calibration” checks, muddy footprints.

Future

  • Potion Hipster: “pre-surge vintage only.”
  • Chronically Lost Adventurer: always looking for “the other dungeon.”
  • Leyline Theorist: insists leylines are giant magical snakes.

11. Stretch Goals & Expansions

  1. Item Infusion (risky upgrades).
  2. Leyline Forecasting UI.
  3. Rival Shops system.
  4. Multi-day Story Arcs from anomalies.

12. Visual & UI Direction

  • Palette: warm woods, glowing blues & purples.
  • UI: parchment + crystal-glow accents.
  • GateDrop Animation: flash → drop → dust puff.

13. MVP Dev Roadmap (2 Months)

Month 1

  • Shop floor & NPC buying loop.
  • Ley-Net ordering + GateDrop delivery.
  • Stock placement & sales tracking.

Month 2

  • Economy balance.
  • Busy/Slow day modifiers.
  • Polish & bug fixes.
  • Basic GateDrop gag events.

Adventurers

Buying Gear

  • "Do you have anything in ‘less stabby yourself’ size?"
  • "Last sword I bought snapped in half… in the sheath."
  • "I’ll take the cheapest health potion you’ve got. Statistically, I won’t need it."
  • "Any discounts for ‘most likely to get eaten by a mimic’?"
  • "Does this armor come in ‘bloodstain camouflage’?"

Selling Loot

  • "Found this in a slime. Still works. Mostly."
  • "If it smells weird, that’s just dungeon air."
  • "Pretty sure this gem is cursed… so… five Leymarks?"
  • "Don’t ask where I got it. Or why it’s sticky."
  • "It only screams when you touch it. Interested?"

Townsfolk

Buying Goods

  • "Is your bread fresh? Yesterday’s batch turned into pigeons."
  • "I’ll take some ley-tea. Strong enough to wake the dead, if you have it."
  • "Does this broom come enchanted or is it just naturally… twitchy?"
  • "You wouldn’t happen to have a ladle that doesn’t bite, would you?"
  • "My cousin swears your candles hum at night. I want three."

Chit-Chat

  • "GateDrop dropped a sheep again this morning, huh?"
  • "I saw an adventurer use a fish as a sword. They won, somehow."
  • "Ley-Net was down last night. Had to talk to my husband. Awful."
  • "The weather forecast says 40% chance of frogs. Bring an umbrella."

GateDrop Couriers

Deliveries

  • "Sign here, here, and here… with your off-hand, please."
  • "Careful with this crate. It’s humming for some reason."
  • "Oh, that? Not on the manifest. Probably fine."
  • "The GateDrop portal’s been moody. This one might smell like cheese."
  • "Our warranty doesn’t cover temporal anomalies."

Small Talk

  • "Had to deliver to a shop on a mountain today. My calves are immortal now."
  • "Portal hiccuped last week and sent me into a birthday party. Not mine."
  • "Don’t stand under the GateDrop. Trust me."
  • "Somebody keeps ordering live crabs to the wrong address."
